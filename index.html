About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the University of British Columbia and BC Cancer, developing agentic AI systems for healthcare — including conversational assistants for cancer patients and clinicians. My work centres on the design, evaluation, and reliability of large-scale agentic architectures and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) systems in high-stakes clinical settings.

At Amazon, I built end-to-end automated evaluation frameworks for a RAG-based customer chat assistant, pioneering LLM-as-Judge and LLM-as-Jury pipelines that delivered scalable assessment of response faithfulness, task completion, and conversational quality — reducing manual evaluation effort by four weeks and improving contact deflection by 2%.

As Agentic AI Technical Specialist at the Vector Institute, I have advised 10+ startups over two years on production agentic AI — spanning multi-hop RAG pipelines, tool-augmented agents, and rigorous evaluation frameworks for reliability and failure-mode analysis.

My research treats evaluation as an end-to-end pipeline property, advancing principled and reproducible methods for agentic systems. I publish in ACM Computing Surveys (IF 23.8), IEEE, and ACM RecSys.

Research Themes
Agentic System Evaluation
Designing rigorous evaluation frameworks for LLM-based agents — measuring task success, reasoning quality, faithfulness, latency, and failure modes in research and production settings.
LLM Reliability & Safety
Building trust in large language models through hallucination reduction, structured reasoning, and LLM-as-Judge methodologies for high-stakes clinical and enterprise deployments.
Clinical AI & NLP
Developing conversational AI for cancer care navigation, mental health assessment, and clinical decision support — with emphasis on responsible, patient-safe deployment.
